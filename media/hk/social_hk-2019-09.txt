+36 =====Shared Post===== The death of a rare dolphin entangled in abandoned fishing nets (Ghost Gear / Ghost Nets). Hong Kong’s population of these rare white dolphins, finless porpoise ... s and sea turtles are on a downward spiral. We can’t afford to loose any of these magnificent marine animals. And we truly can’t afford to have them lost in discarded, abandoned ghost gear. Let alone considering the risk posed to our regular beach and water sport people. Lamma is just one of many island (1 of over 280+) in Hong Kong, and if this small small section of Lamma Island can have so many ghost nets, can you imagine what the remaining islands are like. Or even worse the grand total of abandoned fishing gear in Hong Kong waters alone? It’s the thoughts that nightmares should be made of. So basically both sides of ShamWan and all inside of ShamWan is covered in these abandoned nets. The two peninsulas (see image) (South - Tai Kok ; North - Yuen Kok) have literal 100’s of nets washed up in every flat rocky area on both side of each peninsula. The water in Sham Wan and ShekPai Wan has serious trawler weight/quality nets floating on or just below the surface. Under the water every 100-200 meters we would find more wrapped around rocks below the lower tide line. Whilst costeering around YuenKok I ended up getting entangled in a ghost net (again) currently there are 100’s of these ghost nets around Yuen Kok, Tai Kok and in Sham Wan Bay. Time to stop talking about ghost nets and start removing the ones that actually pose a risk to marine life. You’ll see in the images a skeleton of a chinese white dolphin that we found washed up on the beach entangled in a ghost net. We literally saw about 100 nets, not the simple monofilament ones, but these heavy commercial grade nets on both sides of this point many of them just floating on or just under the surface waiting to trap another dolphin or porpoise. The time for talking has long past. Now is time to do the PADI, AFCD or WWF GhostNet/Abandoned Fishing Gear course, become a safe and professional ghost net recovery diver and some real and meaningful dives. Ultimately it’s is only by proper taining that we will have professional divers doing this work. Time to act !! So more than talk !!
