4. 香港电台新闻(英文) -  (WWF calls for ferry restrictions to save dolphins (17:21)) 2017-12-08 Local   


-----------------------------------------------------------------------------








文字快照 :http://news.rthk.hk/rthk/en/component/k2/1369078-20171208.htm 




The environmental group WWF-Hong Kong suggested on Friday that ferries should be re-routed from waters south of Lantau, or speed restrictions put in place, to help stop an alarming decline in the Chinese white dolphin population.

Latest government figures put the number of dolphins at a record low of 47 this year.

The green group said it conducted an underwater sound study in South Lantau between October 2016 and September this year, collecting the whistles and echolocation clicks of dolphins and finless porpoises, as well as the noise generated by vessels passing through the area.

WWF said this has given them the first full picture of how the mammals use and feed in the busy shipping channel there.

Matthew Pine, a marine scientist and underwater noise specialist, said the mammals are at risk of being hit by ships, and exposure to high noise levels could heighten this risk.

"One of the concerns of long-term exposure, chronic exposure is the possibility of desensitisation," he said.

But he added this concern needs to be confirmed with more data to see if this is the case locally.

The shipping channel between South Lantau and the Soko Islands was found to be a key foraging habitat for the dolphins and finless porpoises. The group said its study suggests marine traffic through the channel has been increasing, and will continue to increase.

Samantha Lee, WWF-Hong Kong’s conservation manager for oceans, said re-routing ferries and setting up speed restrictions could be used to mitigate the impact on the marine mammals.

She said re-routing ferries would add eight minutes to journeys, while imposing a speed restriction would add around 11.5 minutes.

She said both proposals come with potential disadvantages, and she hopes further studies can be done to check which approach would work best.

Hong Kong’s marine environment is in poor health, according to WWF-Hong Kong, which has called on the incoming administration of Chief Executive-elect Carrie Lam to designate 10 percent of the city’s waters as protected areas by 2020.

In its latest study on marine health released on Thursday, World Ocean Day, the group found numerous shortcomings in how marine species and ecological hotspots are being protected. It said previously common species, such as the Chinese White Dolphin, Chinese Bahaba, and Horseshoe Crab, are either now rarely seen in local waters or are under threat.

“We identified that a few threats keep increasing, like heavy marine traffic, marine litter pollution and unregulated fishing practices,” said Angel Lam, a senior marine conservation officer at WWF-Hong Kong.

The group said the population of Chinese White Dolphins in the city’s territorial waters had declined by 60 percent in the last decade. It said the Green Turtle, the only one of seven species of sea turtles worldwide known to breed locally, hasn’t been spotted here since 2012. And it said the Chinese Bahaba population has declined by 99 percent over the past 70 years, thanks to overfishing to fuel demand in the Chinese medicine trade.

The city's marine parks and marine reserve are supposed to help protect wildlife and their habitats, but a Chinese University study commissioned by WWF said these objectives haven't been met.

They say the protected areas lack clear management plans, have minimal stakeholder involvement, and still allow fishing with permits.

Currently, Hong Kong's marine protected areas (MPAs) cover just two percent of the territory's waters. WWF says they should be reviewed, and their total area should be increased to ten percent by 2020.

They've submitted their results to the government and will present which marine ecological hotspots the next administration should turn into MPAs at the end of this year.

立法会审议附属法例，将位于大屿山东北的大小磨刀，列为海岸公园，加强保护香港水域的中华白海豚，补偿兴建港珠澳大桥香港口岸损失的海豚栖息地。渔农界何俊贤指，措施影响行业利益和发展前景，原本计划提出废除令，但一旦通过废除令，可能会令港珠澳大桥不能通车。他批评当局不断扩大海岸公园，减少渔民可捕鱼的水域面积，强调如果当局仍未有诚意，提出支持渔业的措施，必定会反对下一个指定海岸公园的建议。 环境局长黄锦星表示，去年已适度放宽渔民许可证的继承和转让规定，当局亦会研究改善渔业管理模式。
